#### Exercise Description
Running on Time is a form of interval running where activity is controlled by time segments. It involves alternating periods of running and rest or changes in intensity within fixed time intervals. It improves endurance, pace control, and recovery ability.

#### Tips
- Set consistent time intervals (e.g., run / walk).
- Start at a moderate pace, especially at the beginning.
- Control your breathing and maintain rhythm throughout the workout.
- Keep a stable posture and relaxed shoulders during running phases.
- Walk instead of fully stopping during recovery phases.
- Gradually increase intensity as fitness improves.

#### Common Mistakes
- Starting too fast and getting exhausted early.
- Losing track of interval timing.
- Inconsistent pace during running segments.
- Completely stopping instead of active recovery.
- Holding your breath during high intensity.
- Skipping gradual progression in training load.
