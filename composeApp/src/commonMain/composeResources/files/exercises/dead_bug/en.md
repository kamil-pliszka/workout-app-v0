#### Exercise Description
Dead Bug is a core stabilization exercise that targets the abdominal muscles and deep stabilizers responsible for spinal support. It involves alternating the lowering of opposite arm and leg in a controlled manner while maintaining a neutral spine.

#### Tips
- Lie on your back with arms and legs raised, bent at 90 degrees.
- Press your lower back firmly into the floor.
- Lower the opposite arm and leg alternately with control.
- Move slowly and focus on stability.
- Keep your core engaged throughout the movement.
- Breathe steadily – exhale while lowering your limbs.

#### Common Mistakes
- Lifting the lower back off the floor.
- Performing the movement too quickly.
- Lowering limbs too far and losing form.
- Lack of core engagement.
- Poor coordination between arms and legs.
- Holding your breath.
